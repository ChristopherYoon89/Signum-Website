User Guidelines

Welcome to the platform! This application is designed to help you discover, organize and monitor news and other content from thousands of sources on one platform. Our system extracts raw data from the web, processes the raw data, transforms it into a structured dataset, classifies articles into topical categories and assigns relevant tags and keywords.

This guide includes best practices and explains the core features on the front-end and how to use them effectively.

Browsing and Filtering Content by Categories

The simplest way to use the platform is by browsing categorized content. Articles are automatically classified into topical categories based on their content. These categories allow you to quickly filter the overall news stream and focus on areas that matter most to you.

On the Signum News start page, users can scroll to get an overview of the latest news across all categories. At the top of the page, users can open a list of all categories, select one and quickly navigate to the corresponding section. Each category section also includes an upward arrow button that allows users to jump back to the category overview at any time.

To explore a specific category in more detail, users can either click "Read more" at the bottom of a category section or select "Articles" from the right-hand sidebar. Both options redirect the user to the dashboard's Articles section, where the full list of articles is available.

The start page is ideal for users who want to search our news database for a specific topic or to get a quick overview of major developments filtered by categories without setting up detailed preferences.

In some cases, articles may overlap multiple categories. To handle this, our system assigns both a primary and a secondary category. The start page displays only the primary category, while the Articles section in the dashboard shows both the primary and secondary classifications.

Politics

The seven geographical categories primarily focus on political news. The Worldwide category mainly, but not exclusively, covers global politics. This includes international organizations, events involving multilateral cooperation across continents, major bilateral relations and topics not confined to a specific region. Occasionally, the category Worldwide also covers events in countries outside the listed regions, such as major developments in Australia, Canada or Antarctica.

The other geographical categories focus on domestic politics within their respective regions or on bi- and multilateral issues primarily involving those areas. It is important to note that the classification of regions is not solely based on geography but also considers cultural aspects. For example, the Middle East category includes Arab countries in North Africa as well as countries in West Asia, such as Iran.

Economics

The Economics cluster includes the categories Money & Finance, Companies, Energy & Commodities and Bitcoin. Money & Finance covers monetary topics, financial developments and, in some cases, broader economic trends. This category typically focuses on macroeconomic perspectives. The "Companies" category primarily includes articles about specific companies, but it may also cover topics related to management, entrepreneurship, marketing or entire industries. Energy & Commodities addresses topics such as oil, gas, coal, industrial metals, agricultural products, livestock and rare earths. Bitcoin focuses on BTC while occasionally covering broader cryptocurrency or blockchain topics. Articles about cryptocurrencies other than Bitcoin are published only in rare cases.

Society

The Society cluster includes Technology, Science, Culture and Panorama. Technology covers a wide range of tech-related news, including software and apps, hardware, cybersecurity and hacking and engineering developments. Science primarily focuses on scientific discoveries, research developments and academic debates. Culture encompasses social norms and values, art, film, music and occasionally philosophical discussions. The category Panorama is inspired by the German media landscape, where the term refers to general news and everyday events. This includes crime, extreme weather, disasters, human-interest stories, sports and unusual or odd news.

Searching our database

Another simple way to use the platform is by searching the Signum News database. A simple and quick search can be done on the start page of the website. If you want to use more sophisticated search queries, click on Advanced Search which redirects you to a dedicated search page, where you can define multiple filters and search for specific topics.

The search filters include date ranges, included categories, keywords, included or excluded sources and various quantitative indicators such as minimum number of clicks, minimum average user rating, minimum algorithm rating and minimum source rating. Currently, Signum News does not provide an AI based search tool, but rather conducts searches through structured queries and logic based search algorithms.

Custom Feeds

One of the most important features of Signum News is the custom feeds, which allow users to create targeted information streams and define their own algorithms and filters. Custom feeds are displayed in the dashboard under the "Briefing" section, which can be accessed either via the quick-access sidebar at the right or through the dashboard side menu.

When a user creates an account, Signum automatically generates two default feeds that appear in the Briefing: All Articles and My Sources. These feeds cannot be deleted. However, users can remove them from the Briefing via the Settings page.

On the "Add Feed" page, users can create new feeds and customize their filters and algorithm to match specific interests. Each feed can be configured using the following parameters:

  • Feed name
  • Included or excluded categories
  • Included or excluded keywords
  • Included or excluded sources
  • Minimum user rating
  • Minimum algorithm rating
  • Minimum source rating
  • Minimum number of clicks

Users can edit the briefings by clicking on the tools icon next to the feed name in the Briefings section. On the "Edit Feed" page, users can modify previously created feeds and adjust the parameters listed above. In the "Settings" page, users can hide their feeds, reorder their feeds or control how they appear on the "Briefing" page.

Since Signum News is still in its infancy, we currently do not support AND/OR operators, but we plan to introduce more advanced query logic in the future. At present, all filters are combined using AND logic. For example, if an article contains an included keyword but belongs to an excluded category, it will not appear in the feed. Within a specific filter field, the algorithm is based on the OR operator. For instance, if a user selects two included categories the articles are either in one or the other category. The same is true for the keywords and sources filter. If you provide multiple keywords, articles with at least one keyword will be shown.

It is generally recommended, to view the briefings as experimental. Users are supposed to do research on categories and tags and adapt the filter algorithm, if it doesn't work as expected.

Examples

Custom feeds and algorithms can be used in various ways. Let's assume a user is interested in tech and science and might want to create a daily briefing on these topics. To do so, the user navigates to the Briefing section of the dashboard, adds a simple feed and selects the two included categories Tech and Science.

However, the user could also set advanced filters to get a more fine-grained feed. Let's assume the user works as a network administrator and is interested in a feed focusing on IT- and cyber security news and analysis. For this, the user creates a new feed selects Tech as included category and defines included keywords and tags such as IT security, cyber attacks and hacker. This feed would allow the network administrator to keep track of cyber attacks and to get information when and how the servers have to be patched and updated.

The platform can be also used in more sophisticated ways that are optimized for individual professional needs, for instance, for users working in finance. Let's say an investor wants to customize a daily news briefing that helps him or her make better financial decisions. First, the investor adds a feed that streamlines general political developments in different geographical regions and selects categories such as Worldwide, USA and Europe.

In the next step, the investor creates a feed focusing on monetary policy and central banking. To implement this, the user adds a new feed and defines included keywords and tags such as Federal Reserve, Central Banking, ECB, inflation and interests. Moreover, the investor uses the My Sources feed to follow a broad range of news and analysis on financial markets by following specific sources such as Bloomberg, the Wallstreet Journal and Yahoo! Finance. The user could also add a briefing feed and select specific sources to follow preferred outlets or niche markets.

Then, the investor adds and customizes a variety of feeds that allow him or her to follow news articles about specific companies, markets and industries he or she is invested in. To do so, the investor uses the names of companies, brands and their competitors as included keywords and tags, or to a broader view define the industry names. The investor could be specifically interested in the oil market. In this case, he or she could add a feed displaying the Energy & Commodities category or further narrow down the filter algorithm and use the oil tag to specifically filter articles about oil and fossil fuels.

Bookmark Lists for Research

Bookmark lists allow you to save articles into structured collections. These lists are designed for research, tracking topics over time or organizing reading material. Instead of losing important articles in a general bookmark section, you can group them by topic and return to them later. This makes bookmark lists particularly useful for deep research and ongoing monitoring.

These bookmark lists allow users to create multiple bookmark lists, name each list based on a topic or project, add articles from any feed or category and build long-term research collections. For instance, user might create bookmark lists such as "AI regulation research", "crude oil market" or "Epstein research".

The customized lists are available in the "Bookmarks" section of your dashboard. You can create and edit bookmark lists via the top menu. To add an article to a specific list, click the star icon on the article and select the desired bookmark list.

Following Sources

If you regularly read content from specific publishers, you can follow sources directly. Following sources can be used in various ways. First, the Briefing section streamlines in its MySources Feed all articles of the sources you follow. Second, the list of all the sources you follow without displaying their articles can be found in the "Sources" section of the dashboard.

To follow a particular source, you can click on the + symbol next to a source. You can also search for a particular source in the Sources section in the Dashboard side menu and click on the + symbol in the Action column. Another way to follow one or more sources is to create a new feed and define included sources.

Following sources is particularly useful when users trust certain outlets more than others, want to track certain niche industry sources or are interested in following specific regional media outlets. Moreover, the feature allows users to compare coverage across different publishers. If you want more than one following list, you can create different feeds and include specific sources for each individual feed.

Article Ratings

Users can rate articles on a scale from 1 to 5, which allows us to receive user feedback. Ratings help us evaluate content quality and also contribute to personalization. Over time, ratings can be used as a signal for sorting and prioritizing content in your feeds.

Individual user ratings cannot be viewed by the public, but Signum calculates averages across all user ratings for individual articles. Moreover, user ratings are used to calculate an average user rating of sources in addition to our internal algorithm rating of our data pipeline.

Stats

Users can click the graph icon on any article to view a variety of metrics for both the article and its source, including:

  • Individual user rating
  • Average user rating
  • Algorithm rating
  • Article clicks
  • Average user rating of the source
  • Average algorithm rating of the source

We recommend that users rate articles only after reading them and based on their quality. Ratings can be updated at any time, which will adjust the article's average user rating. This average represents the aggregated rating from all users for that specific article.

The algorithm rating is generated by our internal system and is based on automated calculations. The ranking score considers multiple dimensions, including data availability, content quality, topic relevance and ratings of the source. Our algorithm rating of the content quality is mainly taking quantitative factors into account, such as the length of the article, information density or the use of specific words and language.

The article clicks metric represents the total number of unique users who clicked the link; multiple clicks by the same user are not counted. The average user rating of a source reflects the aggregated user ratings across all articles from that source, while the average algorithmic rating of a source is calculated from the algorithmic ratings of all of its articles.
